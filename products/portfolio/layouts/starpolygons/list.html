Star polygons

A star polygon is a geometrical figure formed by connecting equally spaced points on a circumference (p) with straight lines skipping every q-1 points. A common notation for star polygons is called Schl√§ffi notation and it looks like {p/q}. It is assumed that p and q are coprime, meaning the only common divisor they have is 1. Yet it is possible to construct a star with p and q not being coprime numbers, the star polygon like this would be then called degenerate star polygon. To construct a degenerate star polygon start with any point and with each q-th point. When the initial point is reached, choose the next unconnected point and repeat the procedure. The degenerate star polygon consists of q unconnected p/q polygons. A curious property of star polygons: a star polygon of {p/q} looks exactly like a star polygon {p/p-q}.
